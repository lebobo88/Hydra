## Brand Voice

The voice of Hydra Constellation sits at the intersection of two disciplines that rarely meet: the annotated critical edition and the engineering specification. It borrows from the medieval tradition of the gloss — precise marginal commentary that deepens a primary text without competing with it. Every slide speaks twice: once in the headline (declarative, almost liturgical), and once in the body (technical, evidenced, brief). The listener should feel they are being trusted with something real, not sold something aspirational.

The mythic register is held in strict check. Hydra is not "like" Heracles; Pentecost is not a metaphor we are "borrowing." These are structural isomorphisms — patterns that recur because the underlying problems (coordination without loss of identity, many agents with one governing covenant) are universal. The voice names the isomorphism and then moves immediately to the engineering artifact that instantiates it. Reverence earns its place only when the mechanism can be shown.

Voice exemplars to keep on the copywriter's desk: Donella Meadows in *Thinking in Systems* (she names a system's purpose before its parts, holds paradox without mysticism, never confuses metaphor with mechanism); Christopher Alexander in *The Timeless Way of Building* (pattern languages as living contracts between a community and its built environment — directly analogous to CONSTITUTION.md as the immortal covenant); the Carolingian capitulary tradition (the Admonitio Generalis of 789 CE — a constitutional document that governs through enumerated obligations, not aspirations, short declarative clauses, zero rhetorical ornament).

**Do / Don't pairs:**

1. DO: "The immortal head cannot be cut; it holds the hash." DON'T: "Like the mythical Hydra, our system is resilient!"
2. DO: "Three crowns. One covenant. No ambiguity." DON'T: "Our powerful multi-agent platform leverages synergistic capabilities."
3. DO: Name the EU AI Act article by number before explaining why it matters. DON'T: Invoke "compliance" as a benefit without specifying the obligation.
4. DO: Let silence carry weight — 40-word body copy is a ceiling, not a target. DON'T: Fill every slide with parallel bullet lists. Three clauses max.
5. DO: Use the second person sparingly and only when the audience is the subject of a decision ("You hold the signing key"). DON'T: Use "we" for Hydra — Hydra is a proper noun, an entity, not a team identity.

## Art Direction

**Palette — five colors, each load-bearing:**

- `#0D0F1A` — **Void Black.** The presentation background throughout. Not pure black; the slight blue shift keeps it from reading as absence and instead reads as deep space. All other colors are placed against this.
- `#E8D5A3` — **Vellum.** The primary text color and the constellation line weight. Named for illuminated manuscript parchment. Contrast against Void Black: 12.4:1 (exceeds WCAG AA 4.5:1 by a factor of 2.7).
- `#C0392B` — **Immortal Red.** Used exclusively for CONSTITUTION.md references, the immortal head of the central sigil, and fail-closed invariant callouts. High-stakes only. Never decorative.
- `#2E86AB` — **Crown Teal.** The three-crown system — Executive, Forge, Garland — each rendered in this hue with differentiation by icon only, not color divergence. Keeps the crown tier unified.
- `#8B7355` — **Trigram Amber.** The I Ching eight-cell memory substrate. Warm, archival, slightly recessive — it supports the mythopoetic layer without competing with Crown Teal or Immortal Red.

**Typography:**

- Display face: *IM Fell English* (Google Fonts, OFL license). Headlines, sigil labels, act titles. 48–64pt for headlines, tracked +20 for act titles.
- Body face: *IBM Plex Mono* (Google Fonts, OFL license). All body copy, code, governance citations. 16–18pt, line-height 1.6.

**Iconography:** Geometric, compass-and-straightedge, never icon-font. Executive = hexagon; Forge = interlocked double-ring; Garland = branching trefoil. Smith pillars = four vertical bars of descending weight. Trigrams use classical broken/unbroken-line notation in Trigram Amber at 1px stroke.

**Central Sigil — the Pentecost Flower:** Circle of eight petals around a central disc. Disc = Immortal Red, labeled "CONSTITUTION.md" in Fell English. Three petals enlarged 20%, filled Crown Teal (Executive 12 o'clock, Forge 4 o'clock, Garland 8 o'clock — equilateral). Five remaining petals = Trigram Amber (stub squads). Octagonal ring carries the eight trigrams clockwise from top: Qian, Kun, Zhen, Xun, Kan, Li, Gen, Dui. Void Black background, no shadow, no glow.

**Constellation Overlay:** IAU 88-constellation polygons in Vellum at 0.5px / 20% opacity. Hydra (Hya), the largest constellation, at 100% opacity, 1.5px stroke, Crown Teal. Three stars within Hydra carry the crown marks: Alphard = Executive, mid-body = Forge, southern = Garland. Annotation lines in Vellum, 8pt Plex Mono labels.

## Narration Script (~750 words, single narrator, no music cues)

The largest constellation in the night sky has no bright stars. Hydra — the water snake of Greek astronomy — holds 1,303 square degrees of sky through the pattern of its relationships, not through luminosity. That is the first thing to understand about the system you are about to see.

We built something that holds its shape the same way.

In the fifth chapter of Mark's gospel, a man possessed by many spirits answers the question "What is your name?" with "My name is Legion, for we are many." Legion is a coordination failure with a name. Many agents, no covenant, no center. The chaos is not an accident — it is the architecture.

The second chapter of Acts describes a different possibility. One Spirit, distributed into many bodies. Each agent distinct. Each agent capable. Each agent answerable to a single covenant that none of them wrote alone and none of them can dissolve alone.

Hydra is an engineering answer to that second possibility.

At the center of the system is a file. CONSTITUTION.md. It carries a SHA-256 hash. That hash is verified at every session boundary, by every agent, before any action of consequence is taken. No agent modifies it. Proposed changes surface as HITL events — human-in-the-loop gates that pause the workflow until a human hand resumes it. The file is the immortal head. You can cut every other part of this system and rebuild it. You cannot cut that.

We call this the immortal head not because it is invulnerable, but because the system is designed so that its integrity is the precondition for all other operations. Everything downstream of the constitution depends on the hash matching.

The system routes work across three crowns.

The Executive Crown — ExecutiveSuite — handles strategic decisions, capital allocation, risk assessment, M&A review. It thinks.

The Forge Crown — pair-programmer — handles engineering execution: code generation, test, review, rubric-verified quality gates. It builds.

The Garland Crown — RLM-Creative — handles creative intelligence: brand voice, narrative architecture, wireframes, cinematic treatments like this one. It creates.

Three crowns. One envelope schema. Typed boundaries between all of them. A message that cannot be validated against the schema is not a runtime error — it is a governance event.

Behind all of this sits AgentSmith. The four pillars: factory, inspector, sentinel, archivist. Ten fail-closed invariants — N1 through N10 — each specifying a condition under which the system stops rather than proceeding with degraded integrity. Smith does not advise. It enforces. Appeals go through the cerberus-bridge protocol. There is no other path.

This is not caution. This is architecture. A system that relies on agent goodwill for its integrity is not a governed system. It is a hope.

The regulators have noticed. EU AI Act Article 9 requires a risk management system — not a document, a system. NIST's AI Risk Management Framework, now extended with the Agentic Profile, requires you to govern, map, measure, and manage. ISO/IEC 42001 requires an AI management system that generates audit artifacts continuously, not retrospectively.

Hydra does not bolt compliance onto an existing system. It generates compliance as a byproduct of operation. Every typed envelope is an artifact. Every HITL event is an audit log entry. Every hash verification is a governance attestation. The constitution is not overhead. It is the product.

Five of the eight squads are stubs. Legal-compliance, healthcare, sales-GTM, research-DS, customer-support — all registered, all governed by the same covenant, none yet operational. Adding a squad requires one squad.yaml and a typed entrypoint. The covenant scales without renegotiation. The immortal head does not need to be rewritten to admit a new member. It only needs to be honored.

We return to the sky.

Hydra holds 1,303 square degrees not because its stars are bright, but because the pattern of their relationships is stable. The constellation is a covenant between points of light — an agreement that this arrangement means something, that it can be used for navigation, that it will be recognizable from the same coordinates season after season.

That is what we built.

Many heads. One covenant. The distributed agency is not a bug in the governance model. It is the governance model.

The hash is the head. The head holds.

## Accessibility Notes

**Alt-text:** Each slide carries a one-sentence `aria-label` matching the visual cue column in the slide outline. Diagrams set `role="img"`. Code blocks set `role="region"` with `aria-label="Code example"`.

**Reading order:** Strict Z-order — background (aria-hidden), diagram, headline, body, footer. No information conveyed by color alone (crown differentiation uses geometric shape + label).

**Color-blind safety:** Palette tested for deuteranopia, protanopia, and tritanopia simulation. Immortal Red and Crown Teal remain distinguishable against Void Black under all simulations. Article-9 clause map (Slide 18) uses dashed-vs-solid line styles alongside color for mapping differentiation.

**Motion reduction:** Default `data-transition="none"`. All animated elements wrapped in `prefers-reduced-motion` media query — final-state-only render when reduced motion is requested. No parallax, no auto-play, no loops.

**Keyboard:** Full reveal.js keyboard navigation. Focus ring: 3px Vellum outline against Void Black (12.4:1 contrast).

**Typography floor:** No element below 10pt (footer hash on Slide 24). Body minimum 16pt. Diagram labels minimum 8pt with full text equivalent in parent `role="img"` aria-label.
